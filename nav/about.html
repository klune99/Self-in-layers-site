Context & influences

Project Goals

This site extends anatomical study into a digital, research-oriented archive. It emphasizes observation and layered learning — tracing the progression from gesture to structure, surface to depth.

The archive is organized into three main areas:

  • Anatomy Archive — Interactive figure studies with layered overlays revealing gesture, skeleton, and muscle beneath the surface
  • Cabinet of Texture — Process fragments collected from the studio: smudge, paper, lines, medium, residue, and compositional designs
  • Timeline — A 14-week chronological journey documenting the evolution of observation, technique, and anatomical understanding

About the Artist

Hello! My name is Karol Lunenburg I am a Milwaukee-based artist who’s studying at UW Milwaukee. My area of focus is design study, I love to make art; logos, posters, brouchers, cards, and ceramics.

Course Context

This project bridges two disciplines: figure drawing and web design.

Figure Drawing

The artwork in this archive explores line, shape, value, texture, composition, perspective, volume, and space as applied to figure drawing. Through a variety of drawing media and mark-making techniques, this work develops accurate observational skills while learning the basics of anatomy and human proportion. The studies reflect both historical and contemporary approaches to figurative art.

Web Design

Built with HTML and CSS, this site translates studio practice into digital form — organizing drawings, process fragments, and weekly progressions into an interactive archive.
