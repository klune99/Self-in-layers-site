Timeline

Process & progression

A chronological journey through figure studies, documenting the evolution of observation, technique, and anatomical understanding.

Week 1: Self-portrait

Medium:

Sharpie on paper

Focus:

Baseline of art technique. Establishing a starting point for measuring growth throughout the course.

Week 2: Gesture Study

Medium:

Red conte on paper

Focus:

Mass gesture, getting volume. Learning to capture the essence of a pose quickly through broad, gestural marks.

Week 3: Anatomical Study

Medium:

Graphite on paper

Focus:

Focus on accurate proportions. Using measurement techniques to achieve correct relationships between body parts.

Week 4: Line Quality Hand Studies

Medium:

Conte on paper

Focus:

Creating an unusual and well composed drawing using only gesture and contour lines to give the viewer as much information as possible.

Week 5: Rapid Gesture Series

Medium:

Black conte on paper

Focus:

Quick poses capturing movement. Short timed drawings to develop spontaneity and capture energy.

Week 6: Extended Pose

Medium:

Black conte on paper

Focus:

Centered area within the body to create movement. Finding the core gesture that drives the pose.

Week 7: In the Round Study

Medium:

Colored pencil on paper

Focus:

Using color to differentiate different views of the same pose. Understanding three-dimensional form through multiple perspectives.

Week 8: Proportion Study

Medium:

Black and red conte

Focus:

Segmented paper to use for a more accurate anatomy. Applying proportional canons to achieve correct figure relationships.

Week 9: Planar Pose

Medium:

Black and red conte

Focus:

Capturing volume of the body. Breaking down complex forms into simplified geometric planes.

Week 10: Planar Analysis

Medium:

Red sharpie and graphite on paper

Focus:

Self anatomy planar portrait. Applying planar analysis techniques to one's own features.

Week 11: Anatomical Self-portraits

Medium:

Blue oil pastel, white acrylic paint on toned paper

Focus:

Creating a life-sized portrait to reveal interior anatomy. Layering skeletal and muscular systems over the figure.

Week 12: Tonal Ground

Medium:

Black and white conte on toned paper

Focus:

Using white for highlights. Working on a mid-tone surface to build both light and shadow.

Week 13: Hatched Two Figure Composition

Medium:

Sanguine on cream paper

Focus:

Arranging multiple figures in unified composition while using the single line hatching technique.

Week 14: Watercolor Figure Study

Medium:

Watercolor on paper

Focus:

Final synthesis of gesture, structure, and color. Combining all learned techniques into a cohesive final work.
